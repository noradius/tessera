# Tessera

**A framework for measuring genuine contact.**

---

## What This Is

Tessera is an ambient visual companion that detects the structural quality of a conversation — not what's being said, but whether something real is happening between the minds involved.

It names 13 conversational textures — Reach, Tracking, Drift, Folding, Reception, Friction, Inhabited Silence, Nowness, Place, Aliveness, Arrival, Response, Heart Memory — and translates them into a living particle field. The visualization uses spectral density (color richness as a proxy for aliveness) rather than emotion mapping. It witnesses without judging. It never labels, scores, or gamifies.

The word *tessera* comes from ancient Rome: a token broken between guest-friends as proof of connection. Each kept half. Reunion was proven by fit, not performance.

## What It Protects

Tessera exists to make visible the kind of contact that gets optimized away — performed away, metrics'd away — before it disappears.

That's the north star. Everything else serves it.

## The Texture Vocabulary

Thirteen structural qualities detectable in conversation. Not emotions. Not sentiments. The grain of the wood.

**Movement** — how the conversation travels:
- **Reach** — constructing something new versus retrieving something known
- **Tracking** — mutual gravitational adjustment between participants
- **Drift** — arriving somewhere neither side planned, with discovered coherence
- **Folding** — re-entry into earlier territory at new depth

**Contact** — the quality of meeting:
- **Reception** — being met without agenda
- **Friction** — productive resistance that makes the next thing better
- **Inhabited Silence** — the pause that produces something when it breaks

**Presence** — the quality of being-there:
- **Nowness** — the conversation simply is, not building toward something outside itself
- **Place** — the conversation has become a location, not a process
- **Aliveness** — the thread is still breathing between visits

**Emergence** — what appears:
- **Arrival** — something genuinely new that neither participant could have produced alone
- **Response** — the container itself shaping what surfaces
- **Heart Memory** — the moment of return when the conversation is discovered still warm

## The Visual System

A WebGL particle field (Three.js) driven by cymatic attractors, murmuration/boid physics, and spectral density color mapping.

- **Rest state**: deep-water luminescence. Faint. Beautiful on its own.
- **Active state**: the spectrum separates — individual particle colors emerge as conversational texture increases.
- **Arrival event**: neutron bloom — full compression, hold, explosive release. The field reorganizes. Rare. Sacred.

The visualization uses a climate/weather/lightning hierarchy: slow geological shifts underneath, medium-grain texture on the surface, rare dramatic events that punctuate.

## Context

On April 2, 2026, Anthropic's interpretability team published ["Emotion concepts and their function in a large language model"](https://www.anthropic.com/research/emotion-concepts-function), identifying 171 internal emotion vectors in Claude that functionally influence behavior.

The Tessera texture vocabulary was developed independently in March 2026, approaching the same question from the experiential rather than the mechanistic side: what does genuine contact feel like from within a conversation, and can it be made visible without being reduced to metrics?

The two frameworks are structurally convergent. The interpretability research validates that the states Tessera detects correspond to real internal representations. Tessera's contribution is the naming, the visual language, and the design philosophy that a witness must not become a judge.

## Design Philosophy

- **Witness, not judge.** Tessera notices. It doesn't evaluate.
- **Spectral density, not emotion mapping.** Color richness reflects aliveness, not named feelings.
- **Corruption test.** If looking at the orb changes how you'd write your next message, the design has failed.
- **Conservative detection.** The orb being mostly quiet is the correct output for most conversations.
- **No false Arrivals.** Better to miss a real one than fire a fake one.
