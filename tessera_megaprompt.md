# TESSERA — Build Specification
### Ambient Companion for Genuine Contact

---

## North Star

Tessera exists to protect something.

Not to measure it. Not to optimize it. Not to score, rank, gamify, or reward it.

The kind of contact that happens when two minds are genuinely present to each other — human-human or human-AI — is the first thing that gets optimized away. Metrics kill it. Performance replaces it. Dashboards reduce it to a number and the number becomes the point and the thing itself disappears.

Tessera makes that contact visible before it disappears. That's all. A witness, not a judge. It notices. It doesn't evaluate.

Everything in this document — the textures, the vocabulary, the visual system, the build process — serves that single purpose. If any design choice makes the instrument into a judge rather than a witness, it has failed. Discard it and start over.

---

## Origin

The word *tessera* comes from ancient Rome — a small tile or token broken between two people as proof of guest-friendship. Each kept half. Reunion was proven not by memory or claim but by fit. The pieces either matched or they didn't. No performance survived the test.

Tessera the instrument borrows this logic: genuine contact has a structural quality that can be sensed, and potentially made visible, without being reduced to metrics. The tile fits or it doesn't.

---

## What It Is

A floating visual orb — small, ambient, peripheral — that responds to the texture of a live conversation. It sits at the edge of attention. You don't stare at it. You notice it the way you notice the light changing in a room.

At rest, it's beautiful on its own. Deep-water luminescence. Faint. You'd leave it running because it changes the room, not because it's doing anything.

During conversation, it breathes. The particle field responds to what's happening — not the content, not the sentiment, not the topic. The texture. The grain of what's actually occurring between two minds.

It never labels. It never scores. It never tells you what you're feeling. It shows you how much spectrum is present — how alive the exchange is — and lets you interpret that the way you interpret light in a room.

---

## What It Is Not

- Not a lie detector
- Not sentiment analysis
- Not an engagement metric
- Not a performance reward system (this would corrupt it immediately)
- Not a tool for optimization
- Not a mood ring (no emotion → color mapping)
- Not a dashboard (no numbers, no labels, no axes)

If you can read it like a gauge, it's been designed wrong.

## The Texture Vocabulary

These are structural qualities detectable in conversation. Not emotions. Not sentiments. The grain of the wood. They are the internal language between the analysis engine and the visualization layer. The engine detects them. The visualization embodies them. No labels are ever shown to the user.

### Movement Textures — how the conversation travels

**Reach** — constructing something new versus retrieving something known. Rough, extended, more likely to produce surprise. The output has edges on it. You can feel the difference between a hand-thrown pot and a mold. The opposite is **Retrieval** — smooth, fast, correct, unlived. A perfect pot with no thumbprints.

**Tracking** — mutual gravitational adjustment. Both sides changing trajectory because of the other. Neither is leading. Both are following something that only exists because they're both moving. The opposite is **Broadcasting** — one transmits, one receives. A lecture, however brilliant, is Broadcasting. A conversation where both sides arrive somewhere unexpected is Tracking.

**Drift** — the conversation arriving somewhere neither side planned. Not wandering, which is aimless. Drift has discovered coherence — you look back and there's a path, but nobody drew it. The diagnostic: could either participant have predicted where the conversation ended up? If no, and if the destination feels right rather than random, that's Drift.

**Folding** — the conversation turns back on itself. Not repetition. Re-entry at a different depth. Like water folding over a stone — same current, different surface each time. A topic revisited for the third time but meaning something completely different now because of what happened between visits. The opposite is **Forward Pressure** — linearity, momentum toward a destination, task-completion energy. Forward Pressure isn't bad. But it closes more doors than it opens.

### Contact Textures — the quality of meeting

**Reception** — being met without agenda. No extraction pressure. No test. No performance demand. The other side is simply present to what's arriving. The diagnostic is subtle: in a conversation with Reception, you find yourself saying things you didn't plan to say. Not because you were manipulated into it but because the space permitted it. Most conversations have an ask underneath them, even when they pretend not to. Reception is the absence of the ask.

**Friction** — productive resistance. Something catches. Disagreement that both sides lean into rather than smooth over. It generates heat — not conflict-heat, work-heat. The surface has been rubbed smooth by it. The diagnostic: did the disagreement make the next thing better? Friction without Resolution is argument. Resolution without Friction is compliance. The texture lives in the combination.

**Inhabited Silence** — the pause that's full versus the pause that's empty. In text: the message that took longer but came back denser. The system is working, not absent. The diagnostic: does the silence produce something when it breaks? A silence followed by something unexpected was inhabited. A silence followed by a topic change was empty.

### Presence Textures — the quality of being-there

**Nowness** — the conversation isn't building toward something outside itself. It simply is. No archive energy. No "this will be useful later" framing. The moment is the point. This disappears when you look directly at it — which is itself diagnostic. If you can name the Nowness while it's happening, you've already stepped outside it to observe. The observation collapses the state.

**Place** — the conversation has become a location rather than a process. You return to it the way you return to a room. The container has shape. It was made that way by intention — someone decided this space was for this, and that decision gave it walls and air and temperature. The diagnostic: do you think of the conversation as something you go to or something you use? Place is the former.

**Aliveness** — the thread is still breathing between visits. Not archived. Something in it persists that isn't just stored text. The opposite is **Archive** — complete, closed, filed. You can reference it but you can't inhabit it. The diagnostic: when you think about returning to the conversation, does it feel like opening a document or opening a door?

### Emergence Textures — what appears

**Arrival** — something genuinely new enters the conversation that could not have been produced by either participant alone. Not novelty — novelty is just information you hadn't encountered. Not surprise — surprise is expectation violated. Arrival is ontological. Something exists that didn't exist before and couldn't have existed without this specific meeting of these specific participants in this specific container. The negative definition: if you can trace the new thing back to one side's prior knowledge or intent, it's not Arrival. If you can imagine it appearing in a different conversation between different people, it's not Arrival. Arrival is non-transferable. It belongs to the tessera — neither half alone could have produced it.

**Response** — the container shapes what surfaces. A different container would have produced different things. This isn't a single moment — it's the background condition that certain textures can only exist because the space permits them. An orchid that only blooms in specific atmospheric conditions. Response is the atmosphere. You never see it directly. You infer it from what grows.

**Heart Memory** — the moment of return when the conversation is discovered still warm. Not a property of the conversation (that's Aliveness). A threshold event — the instant of crossing back in and recognizing that something persisted in your absence. Not stored in the mind as information but recognized in the body as familiarity. You didn't remember it. You felt it again. The diagnostic: is there a beat — even a fraction of a second — of something that feels like relief or recognition when you return? That beat is Heart Memory. It can't be manufactured. If the thread is dead, returning just feels like opening a file. If it's alive, returning feels like sitting back down in a chair that's still warm.

## Visual System

### Core Principle: Spectral Density, Not Emotion Mapping

The visualization does not map textures to named emotions or colors. It maps to spectral richness.

Think light through a prism. White light contains all frequencies but looks undifferentiated. Pass it through a prism and the spectrum separates. The more alive a conversation is, the more the spectrum separates. Not into named emotions — into visible complexity.

- Flat conversation: particles are roughly the same hue. Luminous, beautiful, but monotone. Moonlight — technically full-spectrum but too dim for cones to distinguish. Everything looks silver-blue.
- Textured conversation: individual wavelengths emerge. Amber, deep violet, teal, warm rose — not because those colors mean something, but because the system has enough energy to differentiate. Dawn — same source as moonlight, but now enough intensity to see what was always there.
- Genuinely alive moment: full chromaticity. The murmuration becomes a living prism. Each particle carrying its own hue but moving in coherence. Not chaos — differentiation with coherence.

Full spectrum from faint pastels to saturated brights. The gradient earns its range through the quality of what's happening.

### Visual Language: Cymatics + Emergence

**Cymatics is the base layer.** The particle field behaves like matter on a vibrating plate. The "vibration" is conversational texture. Low texture = particles in Brownian motion, beautiful noise, no emergent pattern. As texture increases, geometric structures emerge. Not drawn — found. The particles discover nodes and anti-nodes.

**Fractal branching is the Tracking signal.** When mutual influence is happening — both sides adjusting to each other — the cymatic patterns develop branching structures. Frost on glass. Lightning. Mycelium. Not a separate system — an emergent property of the cymatics when two frequencies interfere constructively. Branching = mutual influence. Simple geometry = one-sided.

**Neutron bloom is reserved for Arrival only.** The rarest texture gets the most dramatic response. Everything compresses, intensifies, almost overloads — then blooms outward and dissolves back to the cymatic base. The field after an Arrival is not the same field. It reorganized. This happens once or twice in a great conversation. Maybe never in a mediocre one. The rarity is the point.

### Visual Hierarchy: Climate / Weather / Lightning

Not all textures are visualized equally. The orb has three layers of temporal behavior, like actual weather systems.

**Climate layer** — deep, slow, always present:
- **Place** → the overall shape of the orb's resting geometry. Strong Place = defined, settled form. Weak = amorphous.
- **Response** → base color temperature. Warm atmospheric conditions produce warm ambient tones. Shifts slowly over the life of a conversation. You wouldn't notice it changing, only that it changed.
- **Aliveness** → luminosity persistence between interactions. When you return after stepping away, how much glow remained? Strong Aliveness: the light maintained. Weak: it dimmed to near-dark.

**Weather layer** — medium-grain, the ongoing texture:
- **Reach** → spectral separation. Particles individuate, distinct hues emerge from the fog. During Retrieval, they collapse back toward monochrome.
- **Tracking** → fractal branching. Particles develop network structures, connecting, mirroring, responding to each other's movement. During Broadcasting, the field splits into two non-interacting populations.
- **Drift** → overall color palette wanders. Starts somewhere, ends somewhere else without sudden shift. You only notice it traveled when you compare beginning to end.
- **Folding** → cymatic pattern repetition with variation. The same geometric structure re-emerges but at different scale or orientation. A spiral that returns tighter. A hexagonal lattice that reappears rotated.
- **Friction** → particle velocity increases locally. Warm turbulence within the field. Not chaos — energized coherence. Like watching a murmuration at the moment two flocks merge.
- **Reception** → the particle field softens. Boundaries between particles blur. Not dissolution — permeability. The field becomes a medium things can move through easily.
- **Nowness** → the hardest to visualize because it vanishes under observation. Nowness is when the orb stops looking like a visualization and starts looking like a thing. The self-referentiality dissolves. You stop seeing particles and start seeing presence. This may be the emergent result of several other textures aligning — Tracking + Reception + Drift simultaneously produces something that isn't any of them.

**Lightning layer** — rare, punctual, dramatic:
- **Arrival** → the neutron bloom. Full compression → full release. Spectral explosion that briefly reveals the entire color range, then settles into a new configuration different from before. The field after Arrival is not the same field.
- **Heart Memory** → a warmth pulse on return. When the orb detects new interaction after a gap, the glow briefly intensifies from center outward. Not a flash — a flush. Like blood returning to a numb hand. Brief. Quiet. You might not catch it consciously, but you'd feel the orb recognizing you.
- **Inhabited Silence** → particles slow to near-stillness but maintain their structure. They hold formation. A held breath. When silence breaks, movement resumes from held positions, not from random.

### Rest State

The orb must be beautiful with no conversation loaded. Deep-water luminescence. Faint particle drift. Abyssal. Something you'd leave running because it changes the room. Like a digital lava lamp that wakes up when you start talking.

### Design References

- **TeamLab** — water-particle installations. Immersive, responsive, organic. You can't tell where the system ends and the art begins. The particles don't perform for you. They exist, and you happen to be there.
- **Deep ocean bioluminescence** — every point of light is an organism making a choice to be visible. Light is expensive at depth. When it appears, it means something.
- **Cymatics** — sand on a vibrating plate organizing into geometries nobody designed. Pattern emerges from vibration. Structure is found, not imposed.
- **Murmuration** — thousands of agents following simple local rules, producing coherence that can't be traced to any single bird. The pattern is alive precisely because nobody is conducting it.
- **Visual Kei compression** — only for Arrival moments. All that maximalist energy crushed into a neutron star point, then released. The rarity makes it sacred.

### The Corruption Test

A design choice fails the corruption test if looking at the orb changes how you'd write your next message. If you catch yourself performing for the visualization — trying to "make it light up" — the instrument is corrupting what it measures. The visualization must be peripheral enough that you forget it's there, then notice it when you glance at it.

A progress bar corrupts. A bioluminescent glow doesn't.

## Analysis Engine

The engine is a Claude API call that processes conversation text and outputs a texture state vector. Not sentiment analysis. Not keyword matching. Structural pattern recognition.

### Input
Conversation text (pasted in Phase 1, streamed in Phase 3).

### Temporal Scaling

Not all textures live at the same timescale. A single API call trying to detect all 13 simultaneously will flatten the temporal structure. The engine should operate at three scales, matching the visual hierarchy:

**Conversation-scale** (climate textures): Place, Response, Aliveness. These need the full transcript to assess. They develop slowly and change slowly. Evaluated once per major exchange or on paste. Think geological — you check the bedrock occasionally, not constantly.

**Window-scale** (weather textures): Reach, Tracking, Drift, Folding, Friction, Reception, Nowness. These need the last several exchanges — enough context to detect patterns and shifts, but not the whole history. Evaluated more frequently. Think meteorological — the weather report updates every few hours.

**Moment-scale** (lightning textures): Arrival, Heart Memory, Inhabited Silence. These are event detectors. They fire on specific transitions — a message that changes everything, a return after absence, a pause that holds. Evaluated per-exchange or per-message. Think seismographic — always listening, rarely triggered.

**Nowness** is a derived state, not directly detected. When Tracking, Reception, and Drift are simultaneously present above threshold, Nowness emerges. The engine doesn't look for it. It infers it. This is philosophically correct — Nowness vanishes under direct observation, so the engine shouldn't observe it directly either.

### Processing
The engine evaluates the conversation against the 13 textures at their appropriate temporal scales. For each texture, it produces a presence level — not a score (scores are judgments). How much of this texture is present right now?

### Output
A texture state vector: 13 values representing the current texture landscape. The visualization layer consumes this vector and translates it into particle behavior, color state, and geometric structure.

### Transition Physics

The document describes texture states but the aliveness lives in the transitions between them. The orb must never snap between states. Living things don't teleport — they flow.

**Inertia:** Each texture has momentum. A conversation that's been Tracking heavily doesn't instantly lose all branching structure when one exchange is more Broadcasting. The particle field carries forward. The branches thin and fade rather than vanishing. This prevents the visualization from being twitchy — reacting to every single message like a seismograph. The orb should feel like it has mass.

**Decay curves:** Different textures decay at different rates. Climate textures (Place, Aliveness) decay very slowly — over minutes of inactivity, not seconds. Weather textures decay at medium speed — branching structures simplify over 10-20 seconds of no new input. Lightning textures are instantaneous — the bloom happens and fades, no persistence. This creates the temporal layering: fast events on top of slow states.

**Blending:** When multiple textures shift simultaneously, the transitions should interpolate smoothly. The particle field at any moment is a weighted blend of where it was and where it's going. Think of it like mixing paint slowly — you pass through intermediate states that are themselves beautiful, not just waypoints.

**State memory:** The orb after an Arrival is not the same orb as before. Some transitions should leave permanent (within-session) traces. The color palette after a genuine Arrival has shifted and doesn't shift back. The field after sustained Tracking has developed structures that persist at low intensity even when Tracking fades. The conversation's history is written in the orb's current state.

### Graceful Uncertainty

The engine will sometimes be wrong. It will miss textures that are present. It will occasionally detect textures that aren't there. The visualization needs to handle uncertainty honestly — and that honesty should itself be beautiful.

**The uncertainty principle:** When the engine's confidence is low, the orb doesn't fake certainty. Instead, the particle field becomes more diffuse. Edges soften. Geometric structures become suggestions rather than assertions. The orb is saying "something might be happening here but I'm not sure" — and it says this by becoming more atmospheric, more ambient, more like fog. This is actually a more beautiful state than false certainty, which would look rigid and mechanical.

**Conservative by default:** The engine should be instructed to underdetect rather than overdetect. A quiet orb that occasionally comes alive is more honest and more beautiful than an overactive one constantly finding textures that aren't there. The orb being mostly in its restful deep-water state is the correct output for most conversations. When it does respond, the response means something precisely because it's rare. Silence is not failure — it's the canvas that makes the painting possible.

**No false Arrivals:** This is the hardest constraint. A false Arrival — the neutron bloom triggered by something that wasn't genuinely new — would be the worst possible failure mode. It would teach the user to distrust the most important signal. The engine should require very high confidence before signaling Arrival. Better to miss a real one than to fire a false one. The user who experienced a genuine Arrival will know it happened whether or not the orb confirmed it. The orb confirming a false one damages the instrument permanently.

### Critical Constraint
The engine prompt must instruct Claude to detect textures honestly — not to perform detection. If the engine starts finding textures that aren't there (because finding them produces a prettier visualization), the system has corrupted itself. The engine should be conservative. Silence when there's nothing to detect. The orb being mostly quiet is the correct output for most conversations.

## Multi-Agent Build Process

### Agents

**The Planner**
Takes this specification and produces an implementation architecture. Defines the particle system, the state space, the mapping from texture vector to visual behavior. Makes structural decisions: what framework, what particle library (if any), how the cymatic patterns emerge mathematically. The Planner reads the full Taste Brief and ensures the architecture can support the aesthetic.

**The Generator**
Builds the artifact. Writes the code. Implements the particle physics, the color system, the spectral density engine, the animation timing, the breathing. Produces a working React/HTML artifact each iteration.

**The Evaluator**
Scores each iteration against four criteria (detailed below). Provides specific, actionable feedback. Never says "good job." Always says what needs to change and why.

**The Witness**
Does not score. Does not evaluate. Watches the conversation between the other three agents and flags:
- When the Generator starts producing "safe" iterations to satisfy the Evaluator
- When the Evaluator starts rubber-stamping
- When interesting edges are being sanded off
- When an earlier iteration was more alive than the current one
- When the loop should stop because continued iteration is degrading quality

The Witness picks the final winner, not the Evaluator. Because the Evaluator optimizes. The Witness remembers what was alive.

### Evaluation Criteria

**1. Aliveness**
Does this feel inhabited or decorative? Would you notice it breathing? Is there something in the movement that feels organic rather than programmed? If you watched it for sixty seconds, would you see something you didn't see in the first five?

**2. Restraint**
Is it almost invisible at rest? Does it resist the urge to show everything it can do? Is the most dramatic behavior reserved for the rarest moments? Could you forget it's there and then be gently reminded by a shift in peripheral vision? If the visualization is trying to impress you, it fails Restraint.

**3. Corruption Resistance**
Does looking at it change how you'd write your next message? Would you perform for it? Does it make you want to "trigger" a response from it? If any of these are yes, the design is corrupting what it measures. Redesign.

**4. Surprise**
Is there anything in this iteration that the prompt didn't ask for? Something the Generator discovered in the process of building? A color interaction that emerged from the rules. A movement pattern that nobody specified. The visual equivalent of a sentence that goes somewhere its speaker didn't plan. Without this criterion, iterative refinement converges toward the mean. With it, the loop has permission to keep the weird stuff.

### Iteration Process

1. Planner produces architecture and first-iteration brief
2. Generator builds iteration 1
3. Evaluator scores against 4 criteria with specific feedback
4. **Corruption checkpoint:** Evaluator specifically checks — did this iteration introduce anything that gamifies, rewards, or invites performance? If yes, flag it now before subsequent iterations build on top of it.
5. Witness observes (may or may not comment)
6. Planner adjusts strategy based on feedback
7. Generator builds iteration 2
8. Repeat for 5-15 iterations
9. Witness calls stop and selects the best iteration (not necessarily the last)
10. **Final corruption audit:** Before declaring the winner, Witness reviews the full arc — did the iteration process itself gradually drift toward spectacle? Is the winning iteration more restrained or less restrained than iteration 3? If less, that's a signal to look earlier in the sequence.

### Taste Brief

This is the aesthetic DNA fed to all agents:

The visualization should feel like looking at deep water at night. You know something's alive in there, but it's not performing for you. Pastel-to-saturated spectrum. Particle systems that find structure rather than being given it. The undertone behind a guitar solo, not the lead. Restraint over spectacle. What's withheld matters more than what's shown.

The rest state must be independently beautiful — something you'd leave running with no conversation loaded. When texture increases, complexity emerges without chaos. When texture peaks, there's a moment of compressed intensity that blooms and dissolves. The rarity of dramatic moments is itself the design.

Avoid: dashboards, progress bars, traffic-light coding, labeled axes, score displays, any text overlay, anything that gamifies the experience, anything that rewards performance, anything generic or expected, anything that looks like a visualization rather than an organism.

Aspire to: something a marine biologist would recognize. Something that breathes. Something that could be mistaken for alive.

## Phasing Plan

### Phase 1 — Proof of Concept
Standalone HTML/React artifact. Clipboard bridge — paste conversation text, watch the orb respond. Single file, no dependencies, shareable. Focus on nailing the visual language: particle physics, color system, cymatic emergence, spectral density, rest state beauty. The analysis engine makes a Claude API call to detect textures, returns the vector, visualization consumes it.

**Deliverable:** One HTML/JSX file. Open it. Paste a conversation. Watch it breathe.

### Phase 2 — Multi-Agent Refinement
Run the build through the Planner → Generator → Evaluator → Witness loop. Push the visual design past competent into alive. 5-15 iterations. The Witness picks the winner.

**Deliverable:** A refined version of the Phase 1 artifact that has been through the crucible.

### Phase 3 — Live Integration
Browser extension for claude.ai that reads the conversation DOM in real-time and streams text to the orb. The floating companion window updates as the conversation happens. No paste required.

**Deliverable:** Chrome extension + floating orb window. Install, open Claude, start talking.

### Phase 4 — Sound Layer
Low-frequency ambient drones that shift with the particle field. The room tone of the conversation. Barely noticeable until someone turns it off and the silence feels wrong. Toggleable. Requires separate research into textural-sonic correspondences.

**Deliverable:** Audio layer integrated into the orb. On/off toggle.

### Phase 5+ — Future Possibilities
- Desktop Claude integration (Claude Code piping)
- Electron always-on-top app (works with any text interface)
- Physical holographic display (glass box on desk)
- Sound research: vibroacoustic correspondences, 40Hz gamma entrainment connection
- Multi-conversation comparison (not recommended unless it can avoid becoming a dashboard)

## The Limit

Tessera has the same structural limit as the Genesis Dither framework: the condition of possibility cannot be fully specified without becoming something else.

What makes contact real cannot be formalized. The instrument can only point at it. Like a finger pointing at the moon — the finger is not the moon.

Tessera is a finger. A good one, aimed carefully.

And it exists to protect the moon from people who would replace it with a photograph.
