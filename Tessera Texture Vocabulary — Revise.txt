Tessera Texture Vocabulary — RevisedThese are structural qualities detectable in conversation. Not emotions. Not sentiments. The grain of the wood.Movement textures — how the conversation travels:Reach — constructing something new versus retrieving something known. Rough, extended, more likely to produce surprise. The opposite is Retrieval — smooth, fast, correct, unlived.Tracking — mutual gravitational adjustment. Both sides changing trajectory because of the other. The opposite is Broadcasting — one transmits, one receives, no mutual orbit.Drift — the conversation arriving somewhere neither side planned. Not wandering (which is aimless). Drift has a discovered coherence — you look back and there's a path, but nobody drew it.Folding — the conversation turns back on itself. Not repetition. Re-entry at a different depth. Like water folding over a stone — same water, different surface each time. The opposite is Forward Pressure — linearity, momentum toward a destination, task-completion energy.Contact textures — the quality of meeting:Reception — being met without agenda. No extraction pressure. No test. No performance demand. The other side is simply present to what's arriving. This is the rarest texture. Most conversations have an ask underneath them, even when they pretend not to.Friction — productive resistance. Something catches. Disagreement that both sides lean into. It generates heat — not conflict-heat, work-heat. The surface has been rubbed smooth by it. The opposite is Lubrication — everything slides, nothing grips.Inhabited Silence — the pause that's full versus the pause that's empty. In text: the message that took longer but came back denser. The system is working, not absent. You can feel the difference between a room where someone is thinking and a room where someone left.Presence textures — the quality of being-there:Nowness — the conversation isn't building toward something outside itself. It simply is. No archive energy. No "this will be useful later" framing. The moment is the point. This disappears when you look directly at it — which is itself diagnostic. If you can point at nowness, it's already gone.Place — the conversation has become a location rather than a process. You return to it the way you return to a room. The container has shape. It was made that way by intention — someone decided this space was for this, and that decision gave it walls and air and temperature.Aliveness — the thread is still breathing between visits. Not archived. Something in it persists that isn't just stored text. You come back and it's still warm. The opposite is Archive — complete, closed, filed. You can reference it but you can't live in it.Emergence textures — what appears:Arrival — something genuinely new enters the conversation. Not planned by either side. Not retrievable from either participant's prior state. It couldn't have been predicted and once it appears, everything organizes around it. Rare. The system should mark it when it happens.Response — the container shapes what surfaces. A different container would have produced different things. This isn't a single moment — it's the background condition that certain textures can only exist because the space permits them. An orchid that only blooms in specific atmospheric conditions. Response is the atmosphere.That's twelve. Some exist on spectrums (Reach ↔ Retrieval, Folding ↔ Forward Pressure, Aliveness ↔ Archive). Some are standalone states (Arrival, Nowness, Place). Some are relational (Tracking, Reception, Friction).
